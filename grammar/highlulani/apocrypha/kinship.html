Kinship

Marriage

Ju’idukuru marriage is the life-bonding of two people. It is intended to be eternal, but can be broken if requested by either partner. It is not an exclusive arrangement, and one can be involved in concurrent marriages.

A bonded female is called a julliga married woman, and the male equivalent is a xalli married man. These terms are not used with possessives. On the other hand, the words kitisu wife and silu husband are used with possessives.

Nuclear Family

The words sabba father and piha mother usually refer to biological parents, but can be applied to the main guardians if the biological parents are not around. These are used both with and without possessives.

There are two terms for children: tu offspring for postnatal children, ’appu foetus for antenatal. Both of these are gender-neutral, and are most often used with possessives.

There can be a suffixed ju’i link for spouses’ offspring and parents’ spouses.

sabbaju’ipagu

father-link-1gen

“my step-father”

tuju’iba

offspring-link-2gen

“your step-daughter”

Other kinship terms began as nuclear family names, but were thence extended across a generation. So kaqqa brother and kica sister can also be applied to cousins.
