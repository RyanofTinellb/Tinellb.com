Nouns

Common and proper nouns are open classes; the pronouns are closed.

Proper Nouns

Proper nouns begin with a capital letter in the transliteration.

Ra’ani Ryan (name of a person)

ʔikinnisa Eakins (name of a family)

Sa’imi Caemi (name of a deity)

Tinallibi Tinellb (name of a universe)

ʔirri’a Irìa (name of a city)

Lulani Lulani (name of a language)

Xucinagi Cula The Crackled Egg (name of a story)

Common Nouns

Common nouns can refer to abstract or concrete items.

lu’i love

bata caution

’ahati happiness

caga mouth

purissiji circle

Gender

There are very few words with specific gender, mostly those dealing with family, as well as lulani queen and kimilli king. In these cases, the feminine form is the default, for people of undefined, unknown or non-binary gender.

Compound Nouns

The first part of a compound noun must be a noun, and the subsequent parts specify the meaning of that noun. These parts can be nouns or verbs. The whole noun is usually written without spaces. As discussed above, the syllabification and stress of nonce words are determined on a stem-by-stem basis. However, a compound in common use will start being treated whole.

jifiru’inulli mountain lake ( jifiru’i lake + nulli mountain )

xu’abaju bluebird ( xu’a bird + baju blue )

Person

First person refers to the speaker or speakers, and any group for whom they are speaking.

Second person refers to the listener or listeners, and any group the listener is representing.

Third person refers to any other party. It is not used for the subject of the sentence, whether present or implied.

Number

Pronouns are not marked for number. However, if the pronoun refers to a group that includes more than one of these persons, then additional pronouns can be constructed as follows:

* The second person transitive pronoun fu can be suffixed to the first person pronouns to form first person inclusive plural pronouns.

* The third person intransitive inanimate pronoun pa can be suffixed to any first or second person pronouns, including those with fu, to form other plural pronouns.

sumafu you and I

pixifu to you and me

puttupa from them and me

fillipa they and you

pagufupa of you, them and me

Animacy

Animate pronouns refer to people and other multicellular organisms capable of independent movement.

Inanimate pronouns refer to anything else, such as natural phenomena, plants, fungi, unicellular life and abstract concepts.

The Genitive

The genitive is used to mark a connection between nouns, including one noun possessing another.

Alienable Possession

Possession is alienable when the possessed item can be transferred from one owner to another. Alienable possessions include objects bought or received by a person.

The structure of the possessive phrase for alienable possession is possessor genitive possessed. The genitive marker marks the animacy of the possessor.

julliga disi kulu the married woman, her fork

manafa qa jifiru’i the country, its lake

pagu xissata my musical instrument

disi nukki his strawberry

Inalienable Possession

Inalienable possession refers to items which are unable to be transferred from one individual to another. Inalienable possessions include relatives, parts of the body and objects created by a person.

For inalienable possession, as well as genitive constructions that do not involve literal possession, the structure is possessed-genitive possessor, that is, the genitive marker is an enclitic on the possessed item. This marker is always the third person inanimate genitive pronoun, unless the possessor is a plain pronoun.

kahipagu my arm

sabbaqa lulani the queen’s father

hafiqa nasiʔu the sheep field

Ra’aniqa ʔikinisa Ryan (of the family) Eakins

Sa’imiqa Tinallibi Caemi from Tinellb

Contrasts

Because of the nature of alienability, some contrasts can be made between possessives.

luffupagu my eyes, the ones with which I was born

pagu luffu the eyes I just bought and have in my hand

’asilariba your friend

ba ’asilari the friend you own

hu’asadisi the painting she made

disi hu’asa the painting she bought

Alienable possession is used in a hierarchy when the possessor is of higher rank than the possessed, and inalienable possession when the relationship is the other way around.

lulanipagu my queen

pagu mifasu my vassal

] The pronouns in the object column are used for people and things. The place column pronouns are used for locations in both space and time. The manner column is used to refer to the way in which an action is undertaken, and The state column refers to the condition of a thing. Objects and places are used as nouns, manners and states are used as adverbs. Proximate pronouns refer to objects and places physically or psychologically near the speaker, whereas Distal pronouns are used when the referent is far away. Interrogative pronouns are used in questions, and Negative pronouns are used in negative sentences. Universal means “all” or “every”, Existential means “some” or “any”.

The ‘object’ indefinite pronouns can modify a noun when used as a suffix. When cu’i nothing is used as a suffix, it loses its last syllable. When ’umi something is used as a suffix, it loses its first syllable.

takacu no sound

manafamari this country

lulanimi some queen

kulu’aba every fork

nullisama which mountain?

ʔusu’aba filli’abata every me and every you

The existential markers are used in complements. For instance “I learned what her name is.”

The dative marker is not applied to distal pronouns.

Reduplicated indefinite pronouns with suffixed -ta and are used for emphasis.

sama samata what the hell?

cu’i cu’ita nothing and no one

qa’i qa’ita never ever

’aba ’abata absolutely everything

Attributives

There is a special class of verbs that allow one noun to modify another in a particular way. They are suffixes for the modifying noun. This whole word is then placed before the noun being modified. The modified noun can be proper, common or a pronoun.

qixa doing qilu receiving rusa coming from cani having ra’u being in ji needing na going towards ru called labu resembling nagi becoming funi hu wanting ruku being made of pi giving taku for hu
