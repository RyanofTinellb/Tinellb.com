Syllabary

The Lulani syllabary was developed as a featural script, with similar sounds having similar symbols.

It is written in horizontal rows, right-to-left, and top-to-bottom.

stopvoicednasallateralcentralfricative
labial pPobBOmMUfFv
alveolar tTedDEnNIlLWrRwsSz
palatal cCyjJYxXZ
velar kK>gG<qQA
glottal aiuhHV

Geminate consonants are shown as ;, placed before the geminated consonant.

A central dot / is placed between words, and sentences begin and end with a . symbol.

While their shapes have been modified over time, it is still fairly clear how each phonetic feature has been symbolised.

  • stop: (none)
  • nasal: vertical stroke
  • fricative: arc
  • lateral: swish
  • central: loop
  • labial: low circle
  • alveolar: apex
  • palatal: corner
  • velar: semi-circle
  • glottal: high circle
  • open vowel: (none)
  • front vowel: vertical line
  • back vowel: horizonal line
